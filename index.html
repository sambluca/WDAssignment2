About Me

Hi, I’m Luca and I’m a 19 year old software engineering apprentice at the BBC, currently working in the BBC Sport website team. As well as working I am studying towards a degree in Digital and Technology Solutions at Manchester Metropolitan University, I attend MMU once a week and in the course we are currently studying Web Development, Programming and Business.

I live in Bolton and I have supported the (not so) mighty Bolton Wanderers all of my life, being a season ticket for the majority of it. My Granddad used to play for Bolton and even though I didn’t quite follow in his footsteps, I have played on the Macron (formerly Reebok) Stadium twice now.

I am a huge fan of the game Overwatch, and in my spare time I have been building a website that hits an API and can display any users stats that it gets back. It is build with node-js and I am looking to use React in the future to improve it.

Work Experience

I’ve been a BBC employee since September 2016 and have worked in the BBC Sport Web team since then. I’ve really enjoyed my time in Sport Web as the job has involved a lot of front-end work as well as a lot of behind the scenes back end stuff.

When I first started, the team were just finishing off a new horse racing experience for the website. We have then moved onto a new football experience in which I have been involved with since the start. At the start of the project I was involved in all the ideation sessions and have since moved onto working on the actual product.

During my time in the Sport Web team all the coding I've done has mostly been in pair programming. This is where a more senior developer pairs with me and helps me write code and work through the tasks I get given but more recently I have been getting more of my own tasks to do.

Before this job, when I was studying at college, I worked at an Italian restaurant called Café Italia. I was a waiter/bartender/one time chef and I really enjoyed working part time there.
